Data Structures

Stacks

A stack is an ordered collection of items where the addition of new items and the removal of existing items always takes place at the same end. This end is commonly referred to as the top of the stack, the other being the base of the stack.

They are ordered in a last-in first-out (LIFO) principle. The most recently added items are the ones at the top and will be removed from the stack first whereas the items at the base have been in the stack the longest and will remain there the longest.

Stack oppereations:
Stack() - Creates a new empty stack
push(item) - Adds a new item to the top of the stack
pop() - Removes the top item of the stack and returns it
peek() - Only returns the top item but without removing it
isEmpty() - Checks whether the stack is empty or not (Boolean)
size() - Returns the number of items in the stack
