Problem Solving

Analysing Problems

When approaching a problem the first step is to analyse it, there are many ways to do this and one of the most popular things to do is to compare this problem to previous problems. This technique is called heuristics, for example if you wanted to work out how much money you need to take on holiday you can think about past holidays and how much ou needed on those then compare this trip to those trips and use that information to help solve the problem.

Planning

The next step is to plan a solution to the problem, so this may involve using paper or a board to draw/write possible ways to solve the problem using the information gathered but also considering gathering more data if there is not enough. In the example of the holiday you could research what the prices of local restaurants and super-markets are like and how often you plan on eating out etc. Planning like this will give you a good idea of how best to solve the problem.

Evaluation

After finding a solution the final step is to evaluate your solution, is it the best way of solving your problem or could your solution be refined further to produce a better result. This step may not be needed as your solution might be the only one or the best one but if it isn't always try to think about improving your solution as it will improve your problem solving skills and allow you to solve future problems more easily.
