Elements of Computational Thinking

Back Tracking

Back tracking is an algorithmic approach to solving a problem, it involves starting with the finished result and working backwards to the start to try and find a solution to the problem. You may have many partial solutions to a problem and not be sure which ones will work and which will be useless, backtracking allows you to look at each solution and then abandon them when you find that they do not fit. Backtracking is one of the more situational algorithms so it can't be used on many types of problem.

Data Mining

Data mining is the process of looking through vast amounts of data to try and find links between sets of data, for example looking at weather and ice cream sales throughout a year will give you the conclusion that ice creams sell better when the weather is warmer. Data mining is an extremely powerful tool used widely throughout the business world however it does require vast amounts of computing power so it is only done by big companies with supercomputers.

Perfomance Monitoring

Performance monitoring is used to give designers an idea of how the finished product will work in the real world. Eg: architects will use performance modelling on buildings they have designed and test them against extreme weather simulations, this way they can find out whether their building will be able to withstand the weather and at the same time be safe and not risk anyone's lives or injury. It is also much cheaper than building the building only to find it can't support it's own weight.

Visualisation

There are many situations where it is hard to imagine or picture something in your head however using visualisation things like heat maps can be made that very quickly allow you to see what is happening and where. For example you might want to know what the traffic is like in certain places, a heat map of the area will show you which roads are most coloured and so most busy, this tells you which roads to take and which to avoid very quickly and clearly. Other examples of visualisation can include bar charts, tables, line graphs etc
